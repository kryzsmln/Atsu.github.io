Tokyo 2020 Olympic

Atsu Mizoguchi

Tokyo 2020 Olympic was held from 23 July to 8 August 2021 in Tokyo, Japan. It was postponed for a year due to the outbreak of COVID-19 which made it the first Olympic games to be held in an odd-numbered year. A variety of sports were conducted including five new sports; surfing, climbing, skateboarding, baseball, and karate. The following visualisations displays the comparison of different factors in the Tokyo 2020 Olympic such as countries winning the most medal, individuals winning the most medal, how age impacts the winning of medals, and athlete gender comparison among different sports.

Medal Distribution Map

The map visualisation above represents the amount of medals each country has won with the size of the circle. Each circle is a representation of medals they have won. The country which received the most medals is the US with 113 medals in total followed by China with 88 medals and Japan with 58 medals.

Gender Ratio in Tokyo 2020 Olympics

The pie chart on the left is representing the male vs female ratio. There are duplicates per individual as some athletes attend more than one category. From this pie chart we can tell that the ratio is pretty close to 50:50; however, there are more male athletes than female athletes with 7855 male athletes and 7266 femals athletes.

Age vs Medal

The age with the most gold medals is 24 years old with 73 medals. The age with the most silver medals is 24 years old with 84 medals. The age with the most bronze medals is 27 years old with 85 medals. In fact, the age with the second most bronze medals is 24 years old with 83 medals. This makes 24 years old to be the age with the most medals of 73+ 84+ 85 = 242 medals.

Top 7 Individual with most medal won

The individual who won the most medals is McKeon Emma with 7 medals. She won 4 Gold medals in Women's 100m freestyle·Swimming, Women's 4x100m freestyle relay·Swimming, Women's 4x100m medley relay·Swimming, Women's 50m freestyle·Swimming, and 3 bronze medals in Mixed 4x100m medley relay·Swimming, Women's 100m butterfly·Swimming, and Women's 4x200m freestyle relay·Swimming.
